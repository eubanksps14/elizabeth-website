A Brief Biography

Queen Mary I

Birth and Early Life

  • She was born on February 18, 1516, at the Palace of Placentia in Greenwich, England.
  • She was baptized about a Catholic shortly after birth.
  • She was the only child of King Henry VIII and Catherine of Aragon to survive into adulthood.

Relation to Elizabeth I

  • Queen Mary I and Queen Elizabeth were sisters, born to the same father but different mothers.
  • She was very biased against protestants, something Queen Elizabeth was not.
  • She did not like her sister Elizabeth, so she took any oppotunity she had to lash out against her.
  • Mary I was very much against the Protestant faith, though Elizabeth I was a protestant herself.
  • When the protestants tried to rebel against her, she quickly accused her sister of plotting against her and threw her in jail

Mary's Queenship

  • She killed around 300 protestants
  • She made Catholicism the national religion of England
  • She earned the name "Bloody Mary" after her "war" against the protestant faith
