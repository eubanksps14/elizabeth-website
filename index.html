Queen Elizabeth I

By Patrick Eubanks, Tanner Reich, and Keegan Jurkiewicz

A Brief Biography

Queen Elizabeth I

Birth and Early Life

  • She was the daughter of King Henry VIII and his second wife, Anne Boleyn.
  • She was two years old when her father ordered the execution of her mother.
  • She was tutored and did well in languages and music.
  • For some time she was handed over to her step-mother, who took care of her.

Relation to Queen Mary

  • Mary I and Elizabeth I were sisters.
  • Mary became queen before Elizabeth.
  • Mary I was very much against the Protestant faith, though Elizabeth I was a protestant herself.
  • Thomas Wyatt organized a rebellion against Mary I in 1554 in an attempt to make Elizabeth I queen.
  • Mary had Elizabeth imprisoned due to suspected involvement in the plot.

Elizabeth's Queenship

  • In 1558 Queen Elizabeth took the reigns from Mary I after she died.
  • Elizabeth quickly adressed the issue of religion her sister had created. During her first session of Parliament in 1559, she called for the passage of the Act of Supremacy, which re-established the Church of England, and the Act of Uniformity, which created a common prayer book.
  • "There is one Jesus Christ," she stated. "The rest is a dispute over trifles." The Roman Catholic Church did not approve of her actions, however. Pope Pius V excommunicated her in 1570.
  • Succession became an issue for Queen Elizabeth I, who was later nicknamed "the virgin queen" due to her trouble with marriage.
